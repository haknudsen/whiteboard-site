What is an Explainer Video

Everyone is busy with their jobs and families, so they have less time for other things. Marketers have adjusted their methods according to consumer behavior. Before people created long and detailed videos. Now people may just ignore them. Instead, you need to create videos that catch consumers’ attention to establish engagement. This is why explainer videos have become an ad trend. Explainer videos are short video clips that run for only two minutes or less.

The approach is also quite different from other videos. An Explainer Video will do the following:

  • Use Fun and Creative Themes
  • Use Strategic Scripting
  • Focus on How the Product Works
  • Show How the Product Will Address Viewer's Needs

Below is a list of goals that explainer videos aim to achieve:

Increase Brand Awareness
Explainer videos use creative combinations of visual and audio themes. These themes, or looks, capture your brand’s personality. They are strategically designed so the viewer remembers your business name and message.
Show Your Purpose and Goals
There is no better way to make people understand your goals than to explain them in an engaging way. Explainer videos use straightforward approaches that cut out the fluff. They let the audience process only the important details and avoid confusion.
Improve Your Business’ Online Visibility
You need to attract more traffic to your website. You need to place your videos on important pages. Such as your homepage, product/service page, and landing pages that need traffic.
Provide Useful Information
Explainer videos can provide information that viewers use or refer to when using the product. People are more willing to buy if you present them with information about products’ uses.
Increase Conversions
Your business can only profit from your explainer videos when the viewers convert or buy. You have a successful conversion when videos use effective prompts in the script. These persuade the viewers to click the “buy” or “add to cart” button.

Explainer videos are best for businesses dealing with professional technical products and services. Sometimes classified as intangible goods. Examples of these include financial software, programming services, or a new type of product or service.
